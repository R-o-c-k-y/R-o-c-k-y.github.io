About Us

"Red Fern Spay & Neuter is dedicated towards eliminating homeless cat suffering, euthanasia and overpopulation by providing high quality spay and neuter services to shelters and animal owners throughout the north country region of New York."

Our Clinic

Red Fern Spay & Neuter Mobile Veterinary Clinic was started in 2009 by Dr. Stacy Lambrinos.

The Mobile Veterinary Clinic is an 18 foot custom built veterinary clinic that parks at TRACTOR SUPPLY COMPANY in Plattsburgh, New York.

Dr. Stacy Lambrinos also has a veterinary office at the CATS RESCUE & ADOPTION SERVICES shelter building located at 7196 Route 9, Plattsburgh, New York.

  • The clinic operates by appointment only.
  • Cats are the only animal accepted for spay and neuter surgery.
  • Animals are dropped off the morning of surgery between 8:30 AM and 9 AM and go home the same afternoon.

Meet Dr. Stacy Lambrinos

Dr. Stacy Lambrinos was born and raised in the north country of New York. She grew up on a farm on Rand Hill with horses, cattle, and numerous beloved cats and dogs. Her family owned and operated Gus' Red Hots Restaurant located on Cumberland Head Corners in Plattsburgh, NY from 1951 to 2020.

Stacy worked at her family's restaurant while she completed her undergraduate studies at Plattsburgh State University and worked towards her goal of becoming a veterinarian. She attended Mississippi State College of Veterinary Medicine and received her Doctorate of Veterinary Medicine in May 2008.

Her passion for shelter medicine and spay/neuter surgery was sparked during a spay and neuter day at an animal shelter in Mississippi. She trained closely with a nationally recognized spay and neuter surgeon at the best college in the US for shelter medicine.

In her free time, Stacy enjoys riding dressage and trail riding with her Friesian and off-the-track thoroughbred horses, and spending time with her cats, family, and boyfriend.

The Reality of Overpopulation

Understanding why spaying and neutering is critical to animal welfare.

Two Uncontrolled Breeding Cats Can Produce:

1st Year 12
2nd Year 144
3rd Year 1,728
4th Year 20,736

Millions of unwanted kittens are born in our country each year. During the peak of kitten season (April-September) animal shelters and cat rescues are overworked and overburdened trying to accommodate the large number of cats and kittens in need.

Other cats are less fortunate and left to wander and fend for themselves—making easy prey for larger animals, automobiles, and cruel people. If they do survive these hazards and elements, they soon obtain maturity and give birth to 5 or 6 kittens, to continue the cycle.

Every cat owner whose cat is not spayed or not neutered, and allowed to roam, must accept responsibility for the suffering of homeless cat overpopulation.
